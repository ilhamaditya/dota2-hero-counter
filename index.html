How the Dota 2 counter picker works

Add up to five enemy heroes and counter picks rank instantly — no server, no external API, no AI. Picks that answer more than one enemy rank higher; picks that get countered back rank lower.

Every recommendation explains itself: why it counters that enemy, a lane tip, a teamfight tip, and suggested items — not just a name.

Dota 2 drafting principles

  • Draft around your team's overall win condition, not just a single counter pick.
  • Weigh lane safety early — a hero that "counters" an enemy on paper can still lose the lane if positions clash badly.
  • Track enemy item timings (Blink Dagger, Black King Bar, Aghanim's Scepter) — a counter pick's window can close once key items land.
  • Don't over-commit to countering one hero when the enemy has four others who also need answers.
  • Flexibility beats a "hard counter" that leaves your own draft with no win condition.

Laning counters vs. teamfight counters

Not every Dota 2 hero counter works the same way. A Lane Counter wins the 1v1 or 2v2 exchange early — denying farm and experience — but may fall off by the mid-game. A Disable, Break, or Save counter often matters more in 5v5 teamfights, regardless of how the laning stage went. Each recommendation card separates its laning tip from its teamfight tip so you know which stage of the game the counter pick actually matters in.

Why patch changes affect counters

Valve periodically rebalances hero abilities, base stats, and items. A Dota 2 counter pick that's strong one patch can weaken — or a previously weak matchup can become excellent — after a balance update. That's why this tool displays the patch and review date its matchup data was last checked against, and why counter data should always be treated as guidance to verify against current patch notes, not a permanent fact.

Methodology: how the score is calculated

Counter data comes from two sources, not guesswork. For a core set of 31 commonly picked heroes, counters are derived from real Dota 2 match win-rate data (via OpenDota) — the opponents each hero has actually lost to most often. For the rest of the full 127-hero roster, counters come from a curated reference of durable ability and item interactions rather than live win-rate statistics. Either way, the 0-5 score reflects how one-sided that matchup is.

When you draft more than one enemy, a pick's score adds up across every enemy it counters, drops if an enemy counters it back, and gets a small bonus for answering more than one enemy at once. Filters only narrow which heroes are shown, not their score. Ties break by hero name, so the same draft always gives the same result.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Dota 2 hero counter picker?

A drafting tool that suggests heroes likely to perform well against a chosen enemy hero or draft, based on known ability and item interactions rather than live match data.

How does the counter-pick scoring work?

Each candidate hero gets points for every selected enemy it counters, loses points for enemies that counter it back, and gains a small bonus for countering multiple enemies at once. See the Methodology section above for the full breakdown.

Does this tool work during the draft phase?

Yes — it's built to be used quickly during Captain's Mode or All Pick drafting. Search or tap an enemy hero and recommendations appear instantly.

Is this tool affiliated with Valve or Dota 2?

No. This is an independent, fan-made tool, not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Valve Corporation. Dota and Dota 2 are trademarks of Valve Corporation. See the Disclaimer for full details.

Why do Dota 2 counters change after a patch?

Valve periodically rebalances hero abilities, items, and base stats. A hero that countered another well can lose that edge — or gain a new one — after a balance patch, which is why counter data needs manual review after each major update.

Does this cover every Dota 2 hero?

Yes — all 127 heroes are covered. A core set of 31 commonly picked heroes uses counters derived from real match win-rate data; the rest of the roster uses a curated reference of durable ability and item interactions.

Is my data stored or tracked?

No account or personal data is collected. Your last draft and filter choices may be saved to your browser's local storage only, and the tool works fully without that too.
